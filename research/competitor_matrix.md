# Competitor Matrix for Options‑Surface & Agentic Trading Platforms

This matrix summarises existing products, libraries, and platforms that overlap with **GammaAtlas**’s goals.  Only official documentation, release notes, and project READMEs were consulted; blogs or general reviews were not used.  Links include the date they were accessed (30 May 2026) to aid verification.

## Legend

* **Agentic?** – Whether the product supports autonomous agents that make or suggest trades.  
* **Options‑aware?** – Whether the product exposes greeks, implied volatility, or gamma‑exposure metrics.  
* **Surface mapping?** – Whether it explicitly maps positioning hazards such as gamma pins, squeezes, vol crushes, or liquidity hazards across strikes and expiries.  
* **Workspace/Platform?** – Whether it provides a development environment or data workspace for quants or traders.

### Observations

* **No direct competitor** currently provides a **venue‑agnostic, agentic options surface mapping** product.  Existing gamma dashboards (SpotGamma TRACE, GEX‑Metrix, Option Alpha’s charts) visualise dealer gamma exposure, but none classify positioning hazards (pins, squeezes, vol crush, liquidity) or explain assumptions.  
* Agent‑native trading platforms (AI‑Trader, Public.com Agents) focus on trade execution and copy‑trading rather than surfacing gamma traps.  
* Research‑oriented agent frameworks (TradingAgents, FinRobot) operate on equity or multi‑asset datasets and do not ingest options surfaces.  
* High‑performance trading engines and data APIs (NautilusTrader, QuantConnect, FlashAlpha) provide the raw data and execution infrastructure that GammaAtlas can build upon.  
* The market gap is a tool that synthesises volatility term structure, open‑interest changes, event windows and dealer positioning into actionable, evidence‑graded narratives for human traders.
