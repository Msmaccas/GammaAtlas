# GammaAtlas Pricing

GammaAtlas is an early‑stage product aimed at serious individual traders and small funds.  The following tiers are a suggested framework for licensing the software.  Actual pricing is subject to change as features mature and data provider costs become clearer.

## 1. Community Edition – Free

This edition is designed for enthusiasts who wish to explore the concept of options surface mapping without external data costs.

* Access to the full open‑source codebase under a permissive licence.
* Use of sample fixtures provided in the repository.
* Ability to develop custom providers and agents for personal use.
* No commercial use (non‑commercial research only).

## 2. Professional Edition – Subscription (target: US$99/month)

For active traders and small funds requiring private hazard mapping with custom watchlists.

* All Community Edition features.
* Integration with one data provider of your choice (e.g., ORATS, FlashAlpha, CBOE DataShop) via API keys supplied by you.  Data usage fees are paid directly to the provider.
* Priority support for bug reports and feature requests.
* Access to early beta features, such as enhanced risk models and backtesting modules.
* Commercial use licence for private trading.

## 3. Enterprise Edition – Custom licensing

For funds and platforms wishing to embed GammaAtlas into their own products or provide hazard mapping as a service.

* All Professional Edition features.
* Assistance with on‑premise deployment, containerisation and integration into existing infrastructure.
* Custom provider connectors, including multi‑venue futures and crypto options.
* Dedicated support and Service‑Level Agreements (SLAs).
* Ability to redistribute hazard maps internally or to clients under negotiated terms.

## Additional considerations

* **Data provider fees:** GammaAtlas itself does not supply market data.  Users are responsible for licensing options data feeds from third parties.  Integration work is included only in the Professional and Enterprise tiers.
* **Early‑bird discounts:** Early adopters may receive discounted pricing in exchange for feedback and case studies.  Contact the maintainers to discuss pilot programmes.
* **Open‑source contributions:** Contributors who make substantial improvements to the codebase (e.g., new providers, agents or risk models) may qualify for free or discounted licences.
